I’m Shubham Kumar, a software engineer in Bengaluru working on distributed systems, reliability, and performance.

Over the past five years, I have grown from SDE I to Principal Engineer through three promotions at [LimeChat](https://www.limechat.ai/). I currently work across Agent Studio, Voice AI, and messaging systems that collectively support approximately 1 billion requests each day.

My work is usually found where product behavior meets production constraints: reducing chat-agent latency by more than 50%, building messaging infrastructure for 50 million daily messages and roughly 100 million at peak, and introducing Kafka-backed queues, circuit breakers, and fallback paths to contain failures.

Before LimeChat, I built the backend of a course-hosting platform used by more than 100,000 students across India.

I also enjoy building systems from first principles. Recent projects include an inverted-index search engine, a Kafka-compatible broker, a Lox interpreter, and small database and distributed-systems implementations in Rust and Go.

This site is where I write about the decisions behind that work: what failed, what held up, and what I learned while building it.
